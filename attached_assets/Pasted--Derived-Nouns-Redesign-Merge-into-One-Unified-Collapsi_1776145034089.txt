**Derived Nouns Redesign — Merge into One Unified Collapsible Section**

## Problem

The current derived-nouns area is fragmented and repetitive.

Right now the UI separates related items into multiple repeated sections/cards:

* verbal noun
* active participle
* passive participle
* derived nouns
* additional noun subtypes in separate areas

This creates duplication and weakens the learning structure.

---

## Required redesign

Create **ONE single collapsible section** that groups the major derived noun outputs together.

### Section title

Use a unified title such as:

**المشتقات الأساسية — Core Derived Nouns**

Do not split these into separate collapsible blocks anymore.

---

## Include these six items together in one section

1. **المصدر**
2. **اسم الفاعل**
3. **اسم المفعول**
4. **اسم الزمان**
5. **اسم المكان**
6. **اسم الآلة**

These should appear together as one derivational family.

---

## Layout requirement

Use **one unified comparison layout**, not scattered cards.

Preferred options:

* a single row/column grid
* or a clean table-like structure

### Best behavior

Each category should have:

* Arabic label
* derived form
* transliteration
* short English gloss if available

Make comparison easy at a glance.

---

## Important rule

Do NOT repeat:

* verbal noun / doer / recipient in one section
  and then again under another derived-nouns section

The data should appear once, in one logical place.

---

## Visual hierarchy

### Primary

* Arabic category label
* Arabic derived word

### Secondary

* transliteration
* English gloss

### Tertiary

* optional short note

---

## UX requirement

The user should be able to open one section and immediately see the full derivational picture of the verb.

It should feel like a morphology chart, not scattered widgets.

---

## Remove / simplify

* repeated headings
* duplicate noun cards
* unnecessary separate collapsible blocks for closely related noun outputs

---

## Deliverable

Provide:

1. new unified section structure
2. which duplicated sections were removed
3. screenshot of the final six-item layout
4. confirmation that content was reorganized, not lost
